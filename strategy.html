Market Context

1.1 The US Dental Market

The US dental services market exceeded $186 billion in 2024 and is projected to reach $215 billion by 2028, driven by an aging population, expanded insurance coverage, and growing consumer demand for cosmetic and restorative procedures. There are approximately 200,000 dental practices operating in the United States — roughly 170,000 general dentistry practices and 30,000+ specialty practices spanning oral surgery, orthodontics, periodontics, endodontics, and pediatric dentistry.

The referral channel is the circulatory system of this market. Specialists depend almost entirely on GP referrals for their patient volume. There is no walk-in market for oral surgeons. This creates a structural interdependency that makes referral coordination a mission-critical workflow for every practice on both sides.

Section 1: Onboarding Engine

1.0 Philosophy: Design for Network Density, Not Just Activation

The goal of the onboarding engine is not just to get one practice live. It is to create network density — connected clusters of specialists and GPs who are actively using the platform together. A specialist who onboards alone has limited value. A specialist who activates with 8 referring GPs within 14 days is a node in a growing network.

Every design decision in onboarding should be evaluated through this lens: does this step increase network density?

1.1 Industry Benchmarks

Best-in-class SaaS onboarding benchmarks:

  • Time-to-value (TTV): Under 7 days (world-class: under 24 hours)
  • Activation rate: 60%+ of signups reach the activation milestone
  • D30 retention: 70%+ of activated users still active at 30 days
  • Onboarding completion: 80%+ complete the setup flow end-to-end

CaseLink Target Benchmarks

  • Time from signup to first referral received: < 48 hours
  • Time from signup to activation milestone: < 21 days
  • GP activation rate per specialist invite: > 50%
  • Overall specialist activation rate: > 65%

1.2 The NPI Lookup API

Use the CMS NPI Registry API (free, public, no authentication required).

  • Endpoint: https://npiregistry.cms.hhs.gov/api/?number={NPI}&version=2.1
  • Returns: Provider name, credential type, specialty taxonomy, practice address, phone number, entity type
  • Reliability: Maintained by CMS; updated quarterly; covers all 5+ million US NPI holders
What this eliminates: Typing name, specialty, address, phone number — the four most tedious fields in any registration form. This alone reduces signup friction by an estimated 60–70%. Make NPI the first field in the signup flow.

1.4 Lessons from Successful Two-Sided Marketplace Onboarding

Zocdoc: Built supply (doctors) first, required 100% profile completeness, and assigned human "onboarding specialists" for first 90 days. CaseLink parallel: Assign a human CSM to assist the first 20 specialist accounts manually. Do not rely on self-serve alone in Phase 1.

Doximity: Verified identity via medical license, peer invitations had 4x higher open rates than vendor emails. CaseLink parallel: The referral activity feed is the daily habit that makes the network sticky.

Healthgrades: Pre-populated profiles from public data for a "claim your profile" flow. CaseLink parallel: Pre-populate specialist profiles from NPI data so the flow is nearly complete before the specialist lifts a finger.

1.5 The 30-Minute Onboarding Flow (Minute by Minute)

Minutes 0–2: Account Creation

  • Enter NPI number → system fetches name, specialty, address, phone from CMS API
  • Confirm pre-filled data (one screen, one click)
  • Choose password or SSO (Google, Apple)

Minutes 2–7: Practice Profile Completion

  • Insurance plans accepted (multi-select, pre-filtered by specialty)
  • Preferred referral types accepted (checkboxes)
  • Upload headshot (optional but encouraged — practices with photos have 40% higher GP response rates)
  • Office hours and additional providers

Minutes 7–15: Connect Your Referring GPs

  • Enter up to 10 GP names and email addresses
  • System checks if any are already on CaseLink → surface existing connections instantly
  • AI drafts a personalized invitation in the specialist's voice → specialist reviews and sends with one click
  • Alternative: upload a CSV of GP contacts

Minutes 15–25: First Referral Walkthrough (Guided)

  • System creates a test referral pre-filled with sample patient data
  • Specialist receives, reviews AI-parsed case info, schedules, and sends simulated status update
  • Completion message: "You just completed your first CaseLink referral loop in under 10 minutes."

Minutes 25–30: Integration or Manual Setup

  • One-click integration walkthrough if using Dentrix, Eaglesoft, or Curve Dental
  • Otherwise: set up manual intake form (customizable link GPs can bookmark)

Post-Onboarding (Automated)

  • Day 1: Welcome email with quick-start checklist
  • Day 3: "Dr. [GP] just activated" notification
  • Day 7: Progress nudge toward activation milestone
  • Day 21: Activation milestone celebration + billing notice

3.1 Study Clubs: Economics and Playbook

Study club economics: Average size 12–20 specialists per meeting. Cost for demo slot: $300–500 (sponsor dinner). Expected paying customers per appearance: 2–4. Cost per acquired specialist: $100–200 — the lowest CAC of any channel.

How to Find Study Clubs

  • Ask every new specialist: "Do you attend a study club? What's the organizer's name?"
  • Search Facebook Groups: "[City] dental study club"
  • Dental school alumni groups (Howard, UMD, Georgetown, VCU)
  • AAOMS and AAO local chapter event calendars
  • Ask local dental supply reps (Patterson, Schein) — they sponsor these events and know every organizer

The Study Club Playbook

  1. Get introduced by a member (not a cold pitch to the organizer)
  2. Offer to sponsor dinner for the group: $400 covers most groups
  3. Ask for exactly 10 minutes — not 20, not 30. Respects the group's time and signals confidence
  4. Bring one existing customer for a 90-second testimonial. Peer voice > vendor voice every time.
  5. Demo one complete referral loop live
  6. Close with a specific offer: "Everyone here gets a 30-day trial free, and I'll extend it 30 more days if you hit 10 referrals before billing starts."
  7. Collect business cards or QR code to signup on the spot
  8. Follow up by name within 24 hours referencing something specific from the conversation
  9. Return to the same club 3 months in a row — consistency builds credibility

Section 4: AI Integration as a Moat

4.0 Design Principles: Build the Data Layer from Day One

Core principle: Every data point collected must be captured in a structured, queryable, model-trainable format from Day 1. Retrofitting data collection is expensive and lossy. Build the event schema now, even if the ML models won't be built for 12–18 months.

4.1 Technical Architecture: Event Schema

referral_event {
  referral_id: UUID
  timestamp: ISO 8601
  event_type: [created | received | acknowledged | scheduled | completed | cancelled | no_show]
  gp_practice_id: UUID
  specialist_practice_id: UUID
  specialty: [oral_surgery | orthodontics | periodontics | endodontics | pediatric]
  geography: {zip_code, county, state}
  patient: {age_range, insurance_carrier, insurance_tier, chief_complaint_category}
  case_complexity: [routine | complex | surgical | emergency]

  insurance_verified: boolean
  time_to_schedule: hours
  time_to_complete: days
  outcome: [treated | declined | no_show | referred_elsewhere]
