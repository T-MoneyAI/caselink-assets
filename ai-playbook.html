Why This Matters Now

You don't need to be a tech company to use AI effectively. You need to be a team that's honest about where time gets wasted — and willing to change the habit.

In 2026, the teams that treat AI as a daily tool (not a novelty) are producing at the level of teams 2–3x their size. Not because they're smarter, but because they've stopped doing by hand the things a machine can do in 30 seconds.

CaseLink has a specific advantage: healthcare is one of the most AI-underserved industries. Dental practices aren't being bombarded with AI-powered outreach, AI-prepared QBRs, or AI-written study club content. That means the bar is low, and the differentiation is real. The team that runs on AI before the product goes national will have built a moat out of operational efficiency alone.

A few honest notes before you start:

  • AI is a first draft, not a final answer. Especially for legal, compliance, and anything patient-adjacent.
  • You still need your judgment. AI is good at structure, speed, and synthesis. You're good at relationships and context.
  • It gets better the more specific you are. Vague prompts get vague results. The prompts in Section 8 are designed to be specific.

Prospect Research (2 minutes per practice)

Before any cold call or outreach, spend 2 minutes building a personalized file on the practice.

Step 1 — Gather the basics

  • Go to the practice's website and Google listing
  • Note: specialty focus, number of providers, years in business, reviews, awards, affiliations

Step 2 — Feed it to Claude or ChatGPT

Open claude.ai and paste this prompt:

- Website: [paste key info or URL summary]

status updates. Specialists pay $499/month; GPs are free.

Customer Success — Keeping Specialists Active

QBRs, churn early warning, and re-engagement — all faster.

Quarterly Business Reviews

QBRs feel like a lot of work. They shouldn't be. Collect the data, then run:

QBR Summary Prompt
Here is usage data for Dr. [Name], a CaseLink customer:
- Referrals received this quarter: [X]
- Referral completion rate: [X]%
- Top referring GPs: [list]
- Average time from referral to appointment booked: [X] days
- Estimated revenue from CaseLink referrals: $[X]

Write a 1-page business review that:
- Opens with a clear summary of the value CaseLink created this quarter
- Highlights the top GP relationship by referral volume
- Notes any improvement trends
- Ends with a forward-looking statement about next quarter
- Sounds professional but not robotic
- Is appropriate for sharing as a PDF or reading on a call

Churn Early Warning

Data to Track (in Airtable)

  • Last login date
  • Referrals received: last 30 days vs. prior 30 days
  • GP connections active
  • Any support tickets opened

At-risk flag: Any specialist who hasn't logged in for 10+ days, or whose referral volume dropped >50%, gets flagged for a re-engagement prompt.

Re-Engagement Messages

Re-Engagement Prompt
Dr. [Name] hasn't logged into CaseLink in [X] days. Their last active referral
was [referral detail — e.g., "a crown case from Dr. Smith's office on Feb 3"].

Write:
1. A warm text message from [team member name] — max 3 sentences, not salesy,
   references that specific referral
2. A follow-up email if no response in 3 days — slightly more formal,
   offers a 10-minute check-in call
3. One hypothesis for why they went quiet and what we could offer to re-engage

- Explain the referral coordination problem without being preachy

📄 Sample Article — Use or Adapt As-Is

Every dental professional has a version of this story: A GP refers a patient to an oral surgeon for an implant workup. The referral goes by fax. The specialist's front desk receives it — eventually. The GP's office calls to follow up two weeks later. By then, the patient has called a different specialist.

This isn't a technology failure. It's a workflow failure. And it's happening in practices that are otherwise modern, well-run, and patient-focused.

The referral relationship between GPs and specialists is one of the most important — and most poorly managed — workflows in dentistry. GPs account for the majority of new patient referrals for most specialty practices. Yet the actual mechanics of how referrals are sent, received, and tracked haven't changed meaningfully in decades.

Here are three things practices can do right now:

First, standardize what you send. GPs and specialists often disagree on what a "complete referral" looks like. Create a one-page checklist — the information the specialist needs to accept and schedule the case — and share it proactively with your top referring partners.

Second, close the feedback loop. One of the most common GP complaints is never hearing what happened to the patient they referred. A brief update — "patient was seen, treatment plan is X" — takes 60 seconds and dramatically strengthens the referral relationship.

Third, stop relying on fax and voicemail for time-sensitive cases. For urgent referrals, use whatever communication channel guarantees a same-day response. Increasingly, practices are turning to platforms like CaseLink, which allow GPs to send referrals digitally with case notes and images, and let specialists respond and update status in real time.

The practices that figure out referral coordination will retain their referral relationships. The ones that don't will lose cases to competitors who make it easier to refer.

Product Feedback Loop

Turn scattered customer conversations into structured product input.

Synthesizing User Feedback

After every 5–10 customer calls, consolidate your notes and run:

Feedback Synthesis Prompt
Here are notes from [X] customer calls over the past [time period]:
[paste all notes]

Analyze these and tell me:
1. The 5 most common friction points customers mention
2. The feature or improvement that comes up most often
3. The exact language customers use to describe the main problem
   (quote them directly when possible)
4. Any patterns by practice type or specialty
5. One insight that might be easy to miss

Format as a product team briefing I can share internally.

Feature Prioritization

Feature Priority Prompt
Here are [X] pieces of customer feedback we've collected:
[paste feedback themes or full notes]

Create a prioritized list of product improvements. For each item include:
1. The feature or fix
2. How many customers mentioned it (or estimate frequency)
3. Business impact if we build it (retention / acquisition / efficiency)
4. Rough complexity (low / medium / high)
5. Recommended priority (P1 / P2 / P3)

Present this as a table I can share with the engineering team.

Writing Product Specs

Product Spec Prompt
Here's a feature I want to build, in plain English:
[describe the feature in 2-5 sentences]

Write a product spec that includes:
1. Problem statement — what user pain does this solve?
2. User story — "As a [user type], I want [action] so that [outcome]"
3. Acceptance criteria — specific, testable conditions for "done"
4. Edge cases — what could go wrong or behave unexpectedly?
5. Out of scope — what are we NOT building in v1?

Write it clearly enough that an engineer unfamiliar with CaseLink
could understand exactly what to build.
Section 07

AI Tool Stack

What we recommend, what it costs, and how to get started in 10 minutes.

HIPAA reminder: Do not paste real patient data into any of these tools unless you have confirmed HIPAA-compliant data processing agreements in place. Use anonymized examples in all prompts.

Claude (claude.ai)

Free / $20/mo
Best for: Long documents, nuanced writing, synthesizing feedback, strategic thinking

Get started in 10 minutes: Go to claude.ai, create an account, and try any prompt from Section 8. Claude handles long pasted content better than most tools — paste a full call transcript, a competitor's website, or 10 pages of notes and it won't blink. Claude Pro ($20/month) is worth it for heavy daily use.

Perplexity (perplexity.ai)

Free / $20/mo
Best for: Real-time research with citations, building prospect lists, quick market research

Get started in 10 minutes: Go to perplexity.ai and type a research question as if you're asking a colleague. Unlike Google, it synthesizes results — you get an answer, not a list of links. Great for: "What are the most common complaints oral surgeons have about their referral workflow?"

Otter.ai (otter.ai)

Free / $10/mo
Best for: Auto-transcription of calls, meeting summaries, extracting action items

Get started in 10 minutes: Connect to Zoom or Google Meet. It joins every meeting as a bot and sends you a transcript and AI summary after. Paste the transcript into Claude for deeper analysis. Free tier covers 300 minutes/month.

Fireflies (fireflies.ai)

Free / $10/mo per seat
Best for: Same as Otter, with stronger CRM integrations and transcript search

Get started in 10 minutes: Connect your calendar and Fireflies automatically joins and records scheduled calls. Better for searching across past transcripts than Otter — useful once you have 50+ recorded calls.

Clay (clay.com)

From $149/mo
Best for: Building targeted prospect lists with AI enrichment — LinkedIn, website, and contact data automatically

Get started in 10 minutes: Create a list of "oral surgeons in DC Metro" and Clay will find contact info, LinkedIn profiles, and website data automatically. Saves 5–10 hours of manual research per week at active sales scale. Worth the cost when you're running outreach at volume.

Apollo (apollo.io)

Free / From $49/mo
Best for: Finding contact info for dental specialists, email sequences, prospect database

Get started in 10 minutes: Search by job title ("oral surgeon") + location ("Washington DC"). Export a list of contacts. Use their email sequencing feature to automate follow-ups. Good alternative to Clay at lower volume.

Gamma (gamma.app)

Free / $10/mo
Best for: Turning bullet points into polished presentations in minutes

Get started in 10 minutes: Go to gamma.app, click "Generate," paste the study club prompt from Section 8, and watch it build a presentation. Edit colors and content to match your brand. Produces better-looking decks faster than PowerPoint for most use cases.

Notion AI (notion.so)

$8/mo per member add-on
Best for: AI-assisted note-taking, document drafting, and CRM if your team uses Notion

Get started in 10 minutes: In any Notion page, press the spacebar to open the AI assistant. Ask it to draft, summarize, or improve whatever you're working on. If the team already lives in Notion, this is the lowest-friction AI addition.

Superhuman (superhuman.com)

$25/mo per person
Best for: Email with AI — AI-assisted replies, instant thread summaries, keyboard-first workflow

Get started in 10 minutes: Request access at superhuman.com. Once set up, use their AI reply feature to draft responses to any inbound email in one click. The fastest email workflow available. Worth the cost for anyone on the team who lives in email.

The Point of All This

The tools and prompts in this playbook aren't magic — they're leverage. The team member who uses them will do in 20 minutes what used to take two hours. Over a week, that's an extra day of capacity per person.

Start small. Pick one section this week — probably Sales or Onboarding — and run two or three prompts before your next call. See what comes out. Adjust. Use what helps, ignore what doesn't.

The goal isn't to use AI for everything. It's to stop letting low-leverage work crowd out the high-leverage work: building relationships, closing deals, improving the product, and serving your customers.

You're building something genuinely useful in a space that needs it. Run fast.
